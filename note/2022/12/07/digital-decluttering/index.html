Digital Decluttering

2022/12/07

Today I started looking at all of my digital data in an effort to remove anything that I don’t need anymore. I’m going through service by service and folder by folder.

I have thousands of links saved in Pocket, around a hundred in my reading list in Chrome and that’s not mentioning the thousands and thousands of folders filled with documents, code repos and other stuff I have on my drives and personal cloud.

My idea is to employ sort of a sieve technique where I’ll do a couple of passes on a resource until I’m absolutely sure I want to keep something or not.

  • On first pass I’ll remove anything that clearly I don’t need anymore (if I ever needed it).
    • Things that I encounter and definitely want to save will be moved to a new location.
    • Things that I’m not sure about will be tagged as such and will wait for the second pass.
  • Second pass will happen sometime in the upcoming weeks and the tagged items will be inspected again.
    • If an item is tagged I’ll have to decide on the spot to delete it or save it.

It’s a good way to see what I actually have and what I can live without. Digital data being ephemeral anyway the less stuff I have to worry about, the better.
